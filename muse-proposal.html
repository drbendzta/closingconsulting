Before We Dive In

Why hiring a closer right now would be the wrong move

This isn't just about finding the right person. It's about building the right foundation first — otherwise, no one else will be able to succeed in it either.

The Problem
Great closers want $10K+/month in commission
If you're not yet at $100K/month in collected cash, you simply can't attract that caliber of person. You'll end up with someone inexperienced dropped into a broken system — and wonder why revenue still isn't moving.
The Trap
A broken system makes even good closers fail
Low show rates, unqualified leads, no KPI tracking, no scripts that convert — that environment will make even a talented closer leave fast. And you'll be back to square one, still with a broken foundation.
The Pattern
Founders try to solve a systems problem by throwing people at it
Every time someone leaves, they get replaced instead of fixing the underlying problem. The system stays broken, the ROAS stays low, and you stay stuck. This cycle can go on for years.
The Right Order
Fix the foundation first. Then add people. Then scale.
The founders doing $500K–$1M/month got there because they built the machine before they scaled it. They fixed the operation, got it running profitably, and then brought in the right people to amplify it.
What This Sprint Is About

01 — The Diagnosis

Three things we need to fix.

You have an offer people want, a brand people trust, and more than enough leads to close deals. What you don't have is a system that's optimized to convert these leads into deals — and one that does this without consuming every hour Patrick and Sarah have.

Problem 01
No one is working the leads
There is zero outbound phone or DM follow-up happening. Leads apply, get a couple of emails, and if they don't respond they sit in Asana going cold. When someone falls off at any point in the process — didn't book after applying, no-showed, went quiet after a call — there's no one bringing them back. You're leaving revenue on the table from leads you've already paid for.
Zero dials being made on the entire pipeline
Problem 02
Patrick is spending high-value hours on cold, uncommitted leads
Patrick is creating custom landing pages and recording 15–20 minute personalised Loom audits for people who haven't committed to anything. When they ghost, those are hours gone with nothing in return. The audit model is worth re-evaluating — that level of attention should be reserved for leads who are already serious and pre-qualified, not used as a cold screening step.
Founder time being spent before qualification
Problem 03
Sarah is closing without the right support system
Sarah takes every sales call — on top of program delivery, lead comms, and operations — with no pre-call warm-up, no objection framework, and no filtering of who lands on her calendar. She knows she could close 7–10 a month if the conditions were right. The issue isn't Sarah — it's that the leads reaching her haven't been qualified or pre-framed by anyone.
~3–4 closes/month vs. 7–10 potential
The Result
What all of this creates right now
Revenue is inconsistent month-to-month — no reliable baseline to plan from or scale off
Leads are falling through the cracks at every stage, even when they showed real interest
Sarah and Patrick are exhausted by the sales process — understandably so — and want to hand it off
There's no clean, documented system to hand off to anyone yet — which makes bringing anyone in harder than it needs to be

Before We Talk About Your Setter

Why this has to be a human — not an AI.

You might be wondering whether an AI agent could handle the setter role. Here's why that would actually work against you.

🧠
The Brand Contradiction
"Human connection is the only thing AI cannot replicate." — Muse Storytelling
That's literally on your homepage. Your entire brand is built on the belief that authentic human storytelling beats AI noise. Your leads are filmmakers, creators, and brand builders who have followed Patrick for up to 15 years because they believe in real human connection.

When the very first touchpoint they have with your sales process is an automated AI message, there's a disconnect. It contradicts the thing they came to you for in the first place.

A setter who genuinely understands the Muse brand and can reflect it on a call isn't just a sales tool — it's an extension of who you are. That's what builds trust at the moment it matters most.
"AI is great at volume — sequences, follow-ups, keeping the pipeline warm. That's not what we're replacing. What AI can't do is get someone on the phone who's been ghosting, understand why they haven't responded, and decide in real time whether this person is a fit. For a $10k offer where trust is everything, that moment matters a lot."
